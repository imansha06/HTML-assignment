INTERNET



Understanding how the Internet works in modern life


What is the Internet?

The internet is essentially a vast, invisible web of roads connecting billions of computers and smartphones all over the world. Instead of cars, these roads carry information—like the text you read, the photos you scroll through, and the videos you watch. When you tap a button or type a website name on your phone, you are sending a speedy messenger down these roads to ask another computer somewhere else in the world to send you a file. Within a fraction of a second, that computer packages up the data and rushes it right back to your screen, allowing everyone on Earth to share ideas, play games, and talk to each other instantly.

How the Internet Works

The Internet works using a client-server model where a client requests data and a server responds using HTTP protocol.


What is DNS?

What are TCP and UDP?

TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) and UDP (User Datagram Protocol) are two important communication protocols used in computer networks. They help devices send and receive data over the Internet. Both TCP and UDP are part of the Transport Layer in the Internet model. They control how data is delivered between computers, but they work in different ways depending on the type of communication needed.

Feature TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) UDP (User Datagram Protocol)
Connection Type Connection-oriented Connectionless
Speed Slower Faster
Reliability Highly reliable (guarantees delivery) Less reliable (no guarantee)
Data Order Data arrives in correct order No guaranteed order
Use Cases Web browsing, email, file transfer Gaming, video streaming, voice calls
